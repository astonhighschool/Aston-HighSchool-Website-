Admission Process

Step 1: Enquiry

Submit our digital enquiry form. Our admissions team will provide you with a personalized prospectus and an overview of our curriculum and campus culture.

Step 2: Campus Visit

We believe a visit is essential. Schedule a private tour or attend an Open Day to meet faculty, explore our facilities, and witness student life firsthand.

Step 3: Assessment

Prospective students undergo an age-appropriate entrance assessment and an informal interview with the Head of Admissions to ensure a great academic fit.

Step 4: Enrollment

Upon successful assessment, you'll receive an offer of admission. Finalize the registration paperwork and deposit to secure your place in the Aston cohort.

Common Questions

Admission

What is the admission process?

Parents of prospective students must collect the prospectus and registration form from the school office, fill it in, and submit it in person at the school office.

Is there a written test and interview?

Yes. Students shortlisted by the school must appear for a written assessment and an interview at the school on the specified date and time.

Are parents required to attend the interview?

Yes. Parents must be present during the student interview. A separate parent interview will also be conducted to understand the parents’ attitude, care, and concern towards their child.

How are admissions finalized?

Admissions to all grades are finalized based on the student’s performance in the written assessment test and interview.

What syllabus is covered in the written test?

All students appearing for the written test are assessed on portions from their previous academic year.

How are Kindergarten students assessed?

Kindergarten students are assessed to evaluate:

  • Gross motor skills
  • Fine motor skills
  • Cognitive development
How can parents get admission-related information?

For all admission-related queries, parents are requested to contact the school office directly. All forms must be collected, filled, and submitted in person at the school office.

Does the school reserve the right to admission decisions?

Yes. The school management reserves the right to admit or refuse admission to any child and to revise rules and regulations whenever necessary.

Are verbal agreements accepted by the school?

No. Any communication or request must be submitted in writing. Verbal agreements will not be accepted under any circumstances.

Fees

How is the school fee structured?

Fees must be paid in two instalments only:

  • 50% of the total fees must be paid at the time of admission
Is the fee refundable?

No. Fees once paid will not be refunded if the student leaves in the middle of the academic year.

Can fees be revised?

Yes. School fees may be revised according to the needs of the institution.

What happens in case of non-payment of fees?

Non-payment of fees may result in:

  • The student being debarred from appearing for examinations
  • The student being sent back home

The school will not be responsible for such outcomes.

Are there any additional charges apart from school fees?

Yes. Parents must pay separately for expenses related to:

  • Hospital or medical care
  • Tours, trekking, outings
  • Sports competitions
  • Any other personal expenses
What is the withdrawal policy?

If parents wish to withdraw their ward after admission, the first instalment (School & Hostel fees) will be non-refundable.

Parents must give a three-month prior written notice if they wish to withdraw their ward from the next academic session. Failure to provide notice will result in three months’ full fees (School & Hostel) being charged.

Discipline

What are the language rules in school?

It is compulsory for students to speak in English only within the school premises.

What are the general discipline expectations?

Students are responsible for their behavior both inside and outside the school.

  • Any observed misconduct will invite strict disciplinary action
  • Disrespect, disobedience to teachers or superiors, use of abusive or slang language, or objectionable behavior may result in dismissal or rustication
What is the policy regarding school property?

Any damage caused by a student must be paid for by the parents. In case of collective damage in a classroom, the entire class will bear the cost of repairs.

Are students allowed to bring valuable items?

No. Students must not bring:

  • Mobile phones, Cameras, Pen drives, iPods
  • Cash or other expensive items

If found, a fine of ₹10,000 will be imposed.

What are the rules for leaving the school campus?

No student may leave the school premises with a parent or relative without prior permission from the Principal. Students found outside school premises without permission will face disciplinary action.

What is the policy on substance abuse?

Any student found consuming or possessing illegal substances or engaging in addictive behavior will be immediately rusticated from the school.

What are the rules regarding attendance after holidays?

Students must report back on the reopening day after vacations.

  • A fine of ₹1000 per day will be charged for absence after reopening
  • A fine of ₹500 per day will be charged for late return after holidays
How often can parents meet their child?

Parents and guardians may meet their ward once every 45 days. Only parents or guardians whose names are recorded in the admission form are permitted.

Can parents or relatives meet students without permission?

No. Parents or relatives are not allowed to meet students without prior written permission from the Principal.

Can parents request exceptions to these rules?

No. These rules are followed strictly to ensure fairness and avoid favoritism. Parents are requested not to seek deviations from school policy.

Does the school welcome parent feedback?

Yes. Suggestions made by parents in a cooperative manner are always welcome.
